GSoC 2017 - The spiritual beginning of my journey into Open Source

Auguat 20, 2017 / by Bimalkant Lauhny

On May 5, 2017 I got selected as a GSoC 2017 participant. This was enough to make this year special. It was the end of my 3rd year as a Computer Science undergraduate at NIT Hamirpur . So naturally, GSoC came as an option for summer industrial training. But frankly, it is more than just that for me. Now, since this year's GSoC is coming to an end, I'm going to describe my journey through summers as a GSoC participant.

Image source from It's FOSS

The very beginning...

My dream to be a GSoCer started somewhere around early 2016. In our college, some seniors decided to introduce us to GSoC programme and the world of Open Souce. I was not aware of any of these. These seniors were GSoC participants that year. and were highly respected by juniors. So naturally, as a complete beginner, it was fascinating.

It's really difficult to get started...

At the end of my summer vacations, around early August 2016, I decided to search for a suitable Open Source organization. I wanted to get a kick start as a contributer in any Open Source org, previoulsy selected into GSoC. It seemed really difficult. There were a hell lot of things which were daunting. Firstly, my 2nd year had just ended, and all I had done was Competitive Programming (and the worst thing is that, I still feel just a beginner in that!). No knowledge of softwares, frameworks and languages (only C++!), team work, version control systems like Git and other stuff ( so, now I guess I'm eligible to be called a complete beginner!). Somehow, I selected D Lang org. I visited it's last year GSoC projects page. I don't remember why, but improving the D garbage collector seemed appealing (I must admit that it was too overwhelming to be a beginners' project.)

Now, the aim was clear (atleast, it seemed like that!). I had to learn core D concepts and get a good understanding of Compiler Design (which itself is an undergrad course!). So, things were not easy here. But I had almost 8 months, so no worries. I started reading a lot about D programming and Compiler Design core concepts. This continued till late Semptember 2016.

The phase of Identity Crisis...

OK. I accept that the title is a bit cliched, but bear with me till the end of the post. You'll understand why it is so.

You guessed right, I left the idea of contributing to the D Lang org. I realised how bad I was in Compiler Design. But D Lang was fabulous. No offence, I really love C++, because it was the very first programming language I learned and I was mesmerized at the thought of possibilities. But I must admit, that D Lang is - how I wanted C++ to evolve!. That's certainly my personal opinion, but, I would like to suggest you to just give it a try.

OK, back to the story. Things were becoming bad for me. Companies had started visiting our campus for 3rd year internships. Some of my friends got selected for summer internships. Others had started contributing to Open Source orgs (Hey! I was not jealous. I was just feeling like a loser). I thought - "What a complete waste of two months' precious time!", since I had started earlier that a lot of others, yet I was nowhere.

A few days later, one of my friends suggested me to select a new org with C++ as a required language. He told me that I was good in C++ so, it would help me to get the 'expected start'!. I did what he said. I selected KDE this time. Things went a bit further than earlier, and I chatted with some of the contributers on IRC. I was also able to successfully build Marble, a KDE product. Now, I had to learn Qt 5 for Marble android. Things were going smooth. I had started making androind apps in Qt 5. Remember the title of this para? yeah, I was still suffering from that. So guess what, at mid November 2016, I changed my mind. I again left KDE. I didn't know what kind of start I was expecting. I just kept on thinking that this was not something I wanted. For the first time in my life, I wanted to take a break from coding.

Not the kind'a break you're expecting...

Yes, you read the title right. It turned out to be a different break than the one I expected, and, of course, a lot different than what you're expecting.

Our End-Semester Exams were approaching. Due to the bad phase, I was in no mood of studying for exams. But, I realised, if I would not score good this semester, it would really break me from inside. I was already feeling like a loser for not yet deciding the org in which I wanted to contribute. I didn't even want to think about it, because it reminded me of my utter failure, let alone contribution. So, yes, I tried to study course related subjects (one of which was Compiler Design). This lasted till mid December 2016. After exams, I was at the college for few days. My roommate, who was already a top contributor in FOSSASIA by then, also stayed there. I used to see him contribute with all his might. I also wanted to contribute, but I didn't had the courage to start all again.

And that's how I started Web Development...

I went home during winter holidays (only around 20 days). Now, I had decided to keep GSoC out of my mind. At that time, GSoC seemed not so safe option. I decided to dive into Web Development . One reason for doing this was that, I was totally inspired by my friend. His work in FOSSASIA was Web related stuff (especially, backend). The other reason was that, I wanted to get atleast an internship offer for summer industrial training. So getting one as a Web Developer seemed appealing (What other choices I had!).

Now, only 4 and a half months were remaining for my summer vactions to start. I wanted to build a good background in Web Development before applying for position of an intern. I asked my roommate for some guidance on getting started. He suggested some good websites and learning materials.

At home, I could feel a new excitement. I was going to learn something very common, yet, totally new to me. I was excited to learn how the internet works, how websites are so user friendly and all about the internet. So, I was full with vigour and enthusiasm. I started with some frontend projects and dived deeper into the unknown realms of functional programming in JavaScript. It was amazing. Once again, I was reliving that feeling of awe and amazement that I had, when I had written my first few programs in C++. I totally forgot about the GSoC and my failures associated with it. Even the thought of getting an internship, because of which I started learning Web Development, did not strike my mind even once during the holidays. In between, the results of the semester were announced. And this time I scored the lowest CGPI out of all the semesters. The result was not good. But thankfully, I was preoccupied with learning Web Development, so, it didn't affect me much (although, now it does!). I was so engulfed by the peculiarities and strangeness of JavaScript, that I didn't realise when my holidays came to a hault.

Back to college...

Now that my holidays had ended, I was back in college. I took only a few days to become aware of that same environment surrounded by the familiar air of the tensions and worries regarding my near future. By this time, I had already started focussing on Node.js (a JavaScript based backend framework). Some people take a lot of time in chosing a backend framework. For me, it was easy. I wanted to play more and more with JavaScript, so, Node.js was an obvious choice. But this time, it was not like holidays. Although I was enjoying Node, yet the added heaviness of the strainous environment and the realization that my academics also suffered previous semester, were not letting me fully enjoy. I was happy for my friends who were getting selected for interns, but, at the same time I was worried for my future.

A desperate search...

The title of this para aptly suggests my condition at that time. I had to get an internship offer. I wanted to get rid of the the thoughts filled with tension and guilt for not starting Web Development earlier and grades, ugghh. But luckily, my friends were always there to light me up (no, not with a matchstick! but with their jokes and laughs). They were a true source of happiness and joy, the emotions which were less visible at that time. Of course, there were a lot of my friends who were just like me, desperately searching for an internship. So, atleast I was not alone.

Back to business...

February 2017 had started. I still had no internship offer. The only good thing was, I had successfully completed some beginner projects in Node. So, now I could add the word "Full Stack Web Developer" into my CV and Resume. I knew, I was still not very good in Node, but there were 3 months left for summer vacations. Also, we get to learn during internship too. So, I started applying for more and more intern positions on Internshala and AngelList. One day, while working on project on Node, I thought of looking into GSoC again. I had some Web Development background. I was good in JavaScript. So I thought, may be this time I might get a better start. Now, I was back to the phase of dreaming. Yes, being a GSoCer was a dream that I wanted to turn into reality. But this time, I was trying to think more practically. So, this time, I asked one of my friend and roomate to help me out in selecting an organization. He was already contributing to an Open Source organization MetaBrainz. This time with his help, I decide to contribute to a not-so-big org which was selected as a GSoC 2016 org - LibIndic. This was a smaller org, not very famous like KDE or D Lang. But this org was apt place for me as a beginner. After a about a month, on February 27 2017, the Google was going to announce the name of the selected organizations for this years GSoC. I wanted to be ready till then. I wanted to be sure of my future as an internship. So I started with my full devotion.

The improved me...

This time, the change in me was visible. I was not hesitant anymore. I frequenly asked questions on IRC from the org admins and contributors. In just a few days, I was suddenly the most active member on IRC. I was successfull in persuading one of the org admins to start working on a side-project for the org. The project was fully in JavaScript (What more I could ask for!). We both started actively working on the side project. The organization had applied for GSoC. My mentor wanted to continue the project with me through the summers. I was finally able to show my talent. Everyone in the org was impressed with me. This was really the 'expected start'.

The Final Wave...

Everything was going fine. Things seemed to be in control. As far as I remember, I had succesfully built a few modules and backend of the website. Mid-Semester exams were going to start from 25 February 2017. The wounds of lower grades in last semester were still fresh. I could not take chances. So, on 22nd February 2017, I told my mentor about my exams. He was kind enough to excuse me for a week or two, till exams were over.

Exams were going satisfactory. Actually, I was least concerned about exams. 27 February was near and Google was going to announce names of the organizations selected for GSoC 2017. With each day passing, nervousness was increasing exponentially. A lot of questions were flooding my mind everyday. What if my org would not get selected? I tried to run away from one possibility that this question addressed. I kept on saying - "All is Well". But somewhere inside, I knew that I was not sure about my action-plan if things went south.

Then came the night of announcement. By now you might've guessed the next thing (You remember the title of this para. Don't you?). Yes, LibIndic was not in the list.

What happened next was very rare. My legs and hands and in fact the whole body, in that order, were beyond the feeling of sensation. I know that this might not be easy to grasp for some of you, but, believe me, this is actually what happened. It was not only about 7 months of stress, but all other sorts of emotions that I went through. I realised how it feels when you see your dreams shattering in front of you. I knew I was not prepared for this, and, may be that's the worst thing I did on my part.

Post Announcement Trauma...

It was hard to get over with. 'Things were going fine. Damn Google! The worst thing was that, this was the biggest GSoC with highest number of organizations i.e. 200. Why not LibIndic?' - all such sorts of thoughts along with an overwhelming number of emotions were swirling in my mind. There were 3 more exams remaining, so, I had to keep my calm and continue. I left everything to the almighty's wish.

Why back down?

Karmanyevadhikaraste Ma Phaleshu Kadachana,
Ma Karmaphalaheturbhurma Te Sangostvakarmani
- The Holy Bhagwad Gita

Above this para, is a shloka from Holy Bhagwad Gita. The meaning of the shloka is — 'You have the right to work only but never to its fruits. Let not the fruits of action be your motive, nor let your attachment be to inaction.'

I'll tell you what this shloka has to do with this post. For now let's continue with the real story. So, now exams were over. It was the noon of 3 March 2017, the day of my last mid-semester exam. I had the choice of forgetting about GSoC and try to get some decent intenship offer. I thought deeply for about an hour in silence. My roommates were sleeping so I was not alone, but, there was silence. My actions next were gonna affect my future. It was difficult to get the answers to all the questions in my mind. 'Should I go on with GSoC? There are still 20 days remaining till proposal submission starts. What am I gonna do in just 20 days? Am I worthy enough to be a part of GSoC?' and what not! Then, suddenly I said to myself the same quote from the Holy Bhagwad Gita quotted above in the beginning of this para. Suddenly, nothing seemed more meaningful than this shloka. May be all I could do is to try. I was certain that I'm not bad at trying atleast. So, without further a do, I started something which was totally in my control, the karma (action). I started my laptop almost after a week. I ran my eyes through the list of selected orgs. It took almost 2 hours to choose an organization suitable to my skills. I selected openSUSE . I went through the projects list and decided to work on Jangouts. I tried to keep out of mind the fear of failure. I did't know whether this was going to be my last try, but, I had no hope of getting selected anymore. I kept on telling myself is that I'm going to give my best, rest is up to the almighty, for the action was in my hand and the results were in his.

And I went into the unknown...

The title is very strange but soothing. There is an irony in what happened and the things to come. Earlier, I was confident enough of me getting selected. I had already started watching dreams of being a GSoCer. In some sense, I tried to imagine the future. This illusion was strong enough to give me a huge boost to jump into the race for getting what I wanted. But at the same time, when things seemed different than the dream, I easily lost hope. I thought I was fighting for my dream, but I ignored the reality, the ultimate truth per se. It was not bad to watch dreams, but, it was not good to ignore the possibility of loss. We all do this in some sense or the other. The important thing was that, only when I submitted my self to the reality, and, decided to do what I could, I was able to get that self satisfaction, because, for now I knew, that even if I would fail this time, I shall not stop trying. Although this time the things were a bit clearer, yet I'd realised that the life is unpredictable and unknown, and hence, I decided to embrace 'the unknown'.

For next 20 days, I did all I could. I had a lot of conversations with the project admin and the contributors on IRC. It was difficult to get started. I tried to contribute patches every day. Everyday, I used to dig up the code base more and more and solve the issues on github. I used to sit for long hours continously. I had to do a lot of things simultaneouly. For example, I had to decide the project for my proposal. I selected one of the summer projects. To write a good proposal, one needs to have a clear understanding of how one's planning to achieve the goal of completing project in time. For that, one needs to get a good understanding of the prerequisites of the project. I also had to learn from scratch a few JavaScript frameworks, which were completely new to me. It was tiresome, but, I had to do it. Then came the proposal submission period. I worked on the proposal and at the same time, continued my contributions to the project. It felt like competing with no one, but myself, for I had no time to look what others were doing. Just 3 days before deadline, the project admin asked me to prepare a new proposal on some other summer project which was recently included in the projects list. I literally had no idea what the project was all about, but I agreed and also asked for his help, to which he agreed. Finally, I was able to submit two proposals. That's how it went on for next 22 days till April 25 2017. I wanted to contribute more but the end-semester exams were near. I told the project admin about my exams, to which he wished me good luck!

The happy beginning...

You've guessed from the title, huh! Let's continue just for sake of completion.

"I don't want to see you here until your exams are over!" (add a smiley to it). These were the words of my mentor on IRC in reply to my thanks for selecting me for the GSoC. This happened in the middle of my end-semester exams. So, what now? I had finally achieved what I wanted! But I knew that there was still a lot to come. It was not the ending at all, but a beginning of something 'unknown'. I had no idea how 'Work From Home' works. "How am I gonna work on a project which is completely new to me? Am I going to pass all the evalauations? What if I failed to live up to the expectations of my mentor?" - my mind, once again got flooded with all such questions along with huge waves of mixed feelings. I didn't know the answer to every single question, but, by then, I knew the single answer to every question. "I don't know! I'm just going into the unknown..." - saying to myself, I sat down to prepare for the next exam.

Article By Bimalkant Lauhny

I'm a 4th year Computer Science and Engineering undergrad at National Institute of Technology Hamirpur, Himachal Pradesh, India.
