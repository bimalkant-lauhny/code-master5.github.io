GSoC 2017 - Project Summary

August 22, 2017 / by Bimalkant Lauhny

On May 5, 2017 I got selected as a GSoC 2017 participant under openSUSE . I contributed to the Open Source project Jangouts under the program. This post deals with my work summary throughout this GSoC.

Image source from It's FOSS

The very beginning...

Why back down?

Karmanyevadhikaraste Ma Phaleshu Kadachana,
Ma Karmaphalaheturbhurma Te Sangostvakarmani
- The Holy Bhagwad Gita

Above this para, is a shloka from Holy Bhagwad Gita. The meaning of the shloka is — 'You have the right to work only but never to its fruits. Let not the fruits of action be your motive, nor let your attachment be to inaction.'

I'll tell you what this shloka has to do with this post. For now let's continue with the real story. So, now exams were over. It was the noon of 3 March 2017, the day of my last mid-semester exam. I had the choice of forgetting about GSoC and try to get some decent intenship offer. I thought deeply for about an hour in silence. My roommates were sleeping so I was not alone, but, there was silence. My actions next were gonna affect my future. It was difficult to get the answers to all the questions in my mind. 'Should I go on with GSoC? There are still 20 days remaining till proposal submission starts. What am I gonna do in just 20 days? Am I worthy enough to be a part of GSoC?' and what not! Then, suddenly I said to myself the same quote from the Holy Bhagwad Gita quotted above in the beginning of this para. Suddenly, nothing seemed more meaningful than this shloka. May be all I could do is to try. I was certain that I'm not bad at trying atleast. So, without further a do, I started something which was totally in my control, the karma (action). I started my laptop almost after a week. I ran my eyes through the list of selected orgs. It took almost 2 hours to choose an organization suitable to my skills. I selected openSUSE . I went through the projects list and decided to work on Jangouts. I tried to keep out of mind the fear of failure. I did't know whether this was going to be my last try, but, I had no hope of getting selected anymore. I kept on telling myself is that I'm going to give my best, rest is up to the almighty, for the action was in my hand and the results were in his.

And I went into the unknown...

The title is very strange but soothing. There is an irony in what happened and the things to come. Earlier, I was confident enough of me getting selected. I had already started watching dreams of being a GSoCer. In some sense, I tried to imagine the future. This illusion was strong enough to give me a huge boost to jump into the race for getting what I wanted. But at the same time, when things seemed different than the dream, I easily lost hope. I thought I was fighting for my dream, but I ignored the reality, the ultimate truth per se. It was not bad to watch dreams, but, it was not good to ignore the possibility of loss. We all do this in some sense or the other. The important thing was that, only when I submitted my self to the reality, and, decided to do what I could, I was able to get that self satisfaction, because, for now I knew, that even if I would fail this time, I shall not stop trying. Although this time the things were a bit clearer, yet I'd realised that the life is unpredictable and unknown, and hence, I decided to embrace 'the unknown'.

For next 20 days, I did all I could. I had a lot of conversations with the project admin and the contributors on IRC. It was difficult to get started. I tried to contribute patches every day. Everyday, I used to dig up the code base more and more and solve the issues on github. I used to sit for long hours continously. I had to do a lot of things simultaneouly. For example, I had to decide the project for my proposal. I selected one of the summer projects. To write a good proposal, one needs to have a clear understanding of how one's planning to achieve the goal of completing project in time. For that, one needs to get a good understanding of the prerequisites of the project. I also had to learn from scratch a few JavaScript frameworks, which were completely new to me. It was tiresome, but, I had to do it. Then came the proposal submission period. I worked on the proposal and at the same time, continued my contributions to the project. It felt like competing with no one, but myself, for I had no time to look what others were doing. Just 3 days before deadline, the project admin asked me to prepare a new proposal on some other summer project which was recently included in the projects list. I literally had no idea what the project was all about, but I agreed and also asked for his help, to which he agreed. Finally, I was able to submit two proposals. That's how it went on for next 22 days till April 25 2017. I wanted to contribute more but the end-semester exams were near. I told the project admin about my exams, to which he wished me good luck!

The happy beginning...

You've guessed from the title, huh! Let's continue just for sake of completion.

"I don't want to see you here until your exams are over!" (add a smiley to it). These were the words of my mentor on IRC in reply to my thanks for selecting me for the GSoC. This happened in the middle of my end-semester exams. So, what now? I had finally achieved what I wanted! But I knew that there was still a lot to come. It was not the ending at all, but a beginning of something 'unknown'. I had no idea how 'Work From Home' works. "How am I gonna work on a project which is completely new to me? Am I going to pass all the evalauations? What if I failed to live up to the expectations of my mentor?" - my mind, once again got flooded with all such questions along with huge waves of mixed feelings. But by then, I knew the answer. "I don't know the future!" - saying this to myself, I continued to prepare for the next exam.
